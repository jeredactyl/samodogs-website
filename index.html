Tips for Hiking Responsibly with Your Dog

Ensuring our dogs enjoy the outdoors responsibly helps protect wildlife and preserves trails for all visitors. Follow these key practices:

1. Plan Ahead and Prepare

  • Verify Pet Policies: Confirm dogs are permitted and review park regulations.
  • Choose Less Busy Times: Hike during quieter hours to reduce environmental impact.
  • Equip Your Dog: Bring water, proper gear, and prepare for the terrain.

2. Dispose of Waste Properly

  • Always Pack It Out: Carry and dispose of pet waste properly.
  • Use Waste Bags: Bring biodegradable waste bags and discard them in designated bins.

3. Keep Your Dog on a Leash

  • Follow Leash Rules: Keep your dog on a leash (6 ft. max) to protect wildlife and ensure visitor safety.
  • Encourage Positive Interactions: Prevent unexpected encounters on trails.

4. Respect Wildlife

  • Maintain Distance: Keep a safe distance from wildlife to minimize stress.
  • Protect Sensitive Habitats: Stay on designated trails and avoid fragile ecosystems.

5. Be Considerate of Other Visitors

  • Yield Appropriately: Step aside on narrow trails to allow safe passage for everyone.
  • Minimize Noise: Manage your dog‚Äôs excitement to keep a peaceful environment.

By following these simple practices, you help ensure our natural spaces remain pristine and enjoyable for all.
