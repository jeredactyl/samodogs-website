üå≤ Tips for Hiking Responsibly with Your Dog

Ensuring our dogs enjoy the outdoors responsibly helps protect wildlife and preserves trails for all visitors. Follow these key practices:

1. Plan Ahead and Prepare

  • Verify Pet Policies: Before visiting a trail, confirm dogs are permitted and review specific park regulations designed to protect sensitive ecosystems.
  • Choose Less Busy Times: Opt for quieter times to reduce your environmental impact and have a more enjoyable hike.
  • Equip Your Dog: Bring plenty of water, proper gear, and consider the terrain and weather.

2. Dispose of Waste Properly

  • Always Pack It Out: Carry and dispose of pet waste properly. Dog feces can introduce harmful bacteria and parasites, contaminating soil and waterways.
  • Use Waste Bags: Bring biodegradable waste bags and discard them in designated bins.

3. Keep Your Dog on a Leash

  • Follow Leash Rules: Keep your dog leashed (6 ft. max) to protect wildlife, ensure visitor safety, and respect park regulations.
  • Encourage Positive Interactions: Leashes help prevent unexpected encounters, making trails comfortable for everyone.

4. Respect Wildlife

  • Maintain Distance: Prevent dogs from approaching or chasing wildlife, reducing stress and preventing potential danger.
  • Protect Sensitive Habitats: Keep dogs on trails and avoid areas where wildlife may be nesting or fragile vegetation exists.

5. Be Considerate of Other Visitors

  • Yield Appropriately: Step aside on narrow trails, allowing safe passage for others.
  • Minimize Noise: Manage your dog‚Äôs barking and excitement to preserve a peaceful environment for all visitors.

By following these simple practices, you help ensure that our natural spaces remain pristine and enjoyable for everyone‚Äîincluding your four-legged friends!
